## 3. ACCORDION SECTION 1: VITAL RECORDS (350 words)

### Accordion Header: "Vital Records Translation – Birth, Marriage, Divorce, Death Certificates"

Vital records form the foundation of family sponsorship and residency applications in the UAE. These documents require precise translation because they establish legal relationships—parent to child, spouse to spouse—that determine visa eligibility and inheritance rights.

**Birth Certificates for Child Sponsorship**

Sponsoring a child in Dubai requires a birth certificate translated and attested for ICP or GDRFA submission. The translation must match the passport spelling exactly, include all stamps from the issuing country, and follow the format that UAE immigration officers expect. We handle birth certificates from all countries, including those with complex attestation chains requiring embassy legalization before MOFA attestation in the UAE.

The typical timeline for birth certificate translation is 24 hours for the digital draft, followed by physical delivery with the MOJ stamp. Express service reduces this to 6 hours for urgent ICP appointments. We coordinate with your PRO or immigration consultant to ensure the translation arrives before your submission deadline.

**Marriage Certificates for Spousal Visas**

Marriage certificate translation is required for spousal visa sponsorship, joint property ownership, and certain banking applications. The UAE recognizes marriages performed abroad, but the certificate must be attested by your home country's foreign affairs ministry and then by the UAE embassy in that country before it can be translated and used here.

We guide clients through this attestation chain, explaining which stamps are needed and how to obtain them. If your marriage certificate is already attested, we can complete the translation within 24 hours. If attestation is pending, we provide a checklist of requirements and can coordinate with attestation services in your home country.

**Divorce and Death Certificates**

Divorce certificates affect visa status, custody arrangements, and remarriage eligibility in the UAE. Death certificates are required for estate settlement, repatriation, and life insurance claims. Both document types require sensitive handling and precise translation to ensure legal proceedings move forward without delays.

We treat these documents with the discretion they deserve, offering private consultation via WhatsApp or phone to discuss your specific situation and timeline.

## 4. ACCORDION SECTION 2: IMMIGRATION DOCUMENTS (380 words)

### Accordion Header: "Immigration Documents – Golden Visa, Police Clearance, Bank Statements"

Immigration documents support visa applications, residency renewals, and Golden Visa submissions. Unlike vital records, which are issued once and used repeatedly, immigration documents often have validity periods and must be updated regularly.

**Golden Visa Document Translation**

The UAE Golden Visa program requires extensive documentation, including bank statements, property ownership deeds, investment certificates, and educational credentials. Each document must be translated, attested, and formatted according to the specific Golden Visa category you're applying under—investor, entrepreneur, specialized talent, or outstanding student.

We've developed a Golden Visa checklist that maps document requirements to each category, helping you understand which translations are mandatory and which are supplementary. For property investors, this includes title deeds and mortgage documents. For entrepreneurs, this includes business licenses and revenue statements. For specialized talent, this includes employment contracts and salary certificates.

The Golden Visa application process is time-sensitive, with documents expiring if not submitted within specific windows. We prioritize Golden Visa translations to meet these deadlines, offering express service for urgent applications.

**Police Clearance Certificates**

Police clearance certificates (PCCs) or good conduct certificates are required for employment visas, residency renewals, and certain professional licenses. These documents must be issued within the last six months and attested by your home country's foreign affairs ministry before translation.

We translate PCCs from all countries, including those with non-standard formats or multiple issuing authorities. The translation must include all stamps, signatures, and reference numbers to be accepted by UAE immigration authorities.

**Bank Statements and Financial Documents**

Bank statements are required for Golden Visa applications, family sponsorship income verification, and certain business license applications. The UAE typically requires six months of consecutive statements showing minimum balance thresholds.

We translate bank statements while preserving the original formatting, including account numbers, transaction details, and bank letterhead. The translation includes a certification statement confirming accuracy, which immigration officers use to verify the document's authenticity.

**Driving Licenses**

International driving license translation is required for RTA file opening and license conversion in Dubai. The translation must include all license categories, restrictions, and validity dates. We handle driving licenses from all countries, including those with non-Latin scripts or complex endorsement systems.

## 5. ACCORDION SECTION 3: ACADEMIC CREDENTIALS (340 words)

### Accordion Header: "Academic Credentials – Degrees, Transcripts, School Records"

Academic document translation supports school enrollment, university admissions, professional licensing, and employment verification in the UAE. These documents require precise translation because they determine educational equivalency and qualification recognition.

**University Degrees and Diplomas**

University degree translation is required for Ministry of Education equivalency certificates, professional licensing, and employment in regulated industries like healthcare and engineering. The translation must include the degree title, major, graduation date, and university seal.

The UAE recognizes degrees from accredited international universities, but the degree must be attested by your home country's education ministry and foreign affairs ministry before it can be used here. We coordinate with attestation services to ensure your degree follows the correct attestation chain before translation.

**School Transcripts for Enrollment**

School transcript translation is required when transferring children between international schools in Dubai or enrolling in UAE schools for the first time. The Knowledge and Human Development Authority (KHDA) requires transcripts showing grade progression, subjects studied, and grading scales.

We translate transcripts while preserving the original formatting, including GPA calculations, credit hours, and teacher signatures. The translation includes explanatory notes for grading systems that differ from UAE standards, helping school administrators understand the student's academic background.

**Professional Certificates**

Professional certificates and training credentials support licensing applications in regulated industries. These documents must be translated and attested to verify qualifications for positions requiring specific certifications.

We handle professional certificates from all industries, including medical licenses, engineering certifications, teaching credentials, and trade qualifications. The translation includes all course details, examination results, and issuing authority information.

**Equivalency Certificate Support**

The UAE Ministry of Education issues equivalency certificates that recognize foreign degrees as equivalent to UAE qualifications. This process requires translated and attested academic documents. We provide guidance on the equivalency application process, including which documents need translation and which attestations are required.

Our service includes document review to ensure your academic credentials meet Ministry of Education requirements before you begin the equivalency application.

## 7. FAQ SECTION (8 Questions, 420 words)

### Section Label: "Common Questions"
### H2: Personal Document Translation FAQ

**Q: Do I need to attest my documents before translation?**

It depends on the document type and your intended use. Birth certificates, marriage certificates, and academic degrees typically require attestation from your home country before they can be used in the UAE. Police clearance certificates and bank statements usually do not require attestation before translation. We review your specific documents during the pre-validation process and advise you on attestation requirements.

**Q: How long does personal document translation take?**

Standard service delivers digital drafts within 24 hours. Express service reduces this to 6 hours. VIP Concierge service delivers within 1 hour for standard documents. Physical delivery adds 24 hours for standard service, 12 hours for express, and can be same-day for VIP service depending on your location.

**Q: Will my translation be accepted by ICP and GDRFA?**

Yes. All our translations are performed by MOJ-licensed partners and include the legal stamp required for government submissions. We pre-validate documents to ensure they meet the specific requirements of ICP (Dubai), GDRFA (other emirates), and MOFA for federal applications.

**Q: Can you translate documents in languages other than English and Arabic?**

Yes. We handle documents in all major languages, including French, Spanish, German, Russian, Chinese, Hindi, Urdu, and Tagalog. The translation process involves two steps: first translating to English or Arabic, then certifying with the MOJ stamp. Some rare languages may require additional time to source qualified translators.

**Q: What if my document has errors or missing information?**

We flag any discrepancies during the pre-validation process. If your document has spelling errors, missing stamps, or unclear information, we notify you before beginning translation. In some cases, you may need to obtain a corrected document from the issuing authority before translation can proceed.

**Q: Do you deliver to locations outside Dubai?**

Yes. We deliver across all UAE emirates, including Abu Dhabi, Sharjah, Ajman, Ras Al Khaimah, Fujairah, and Umm Al Quwain. Delivery times vary by location, with same-day service available in Dubai and Abu Dhabi, and next-day service in other emirates.

**Q: Can you help with document attestation?**

We provide guidance on attestation requirements and can coordinate with attestation services in the UAE and abroad. While we don't perform attestation directly, we work with trusted partners who handle MOFA attestation, embassy legalization, and home country attestation chains.

**Q: What payment methods do you accept?**

We accept bank transfer, credit card, and cash on delivery for physical documents. Payment is required after you approve the digital draft and before we proceed with physical printing and stamping. We provide detailed invoices for all transactions.
